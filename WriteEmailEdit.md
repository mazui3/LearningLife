# 1

## 逐句讲解

### 第一句
> ❌ I am writing to express my concern about the emergency situation that I accidentaly left my bag at your restaurant yesterday.

**问题：**
- `accidentaly` → 拼写错误，正确是 **accidentally**（两个 l）。
- `express my concern about the emergency situation that I left...` → 语意别扭。你并不是担心“紧急情况”，而是因为包丢了感到担心。用 `because` 更自然。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I am writing to express my concern because I accidentally left my bag at your restaurant yesterday.**

---

### 第二句
> ❌ My friend and I dinned in around 6 pm in your restaurant, at a table near the window with two sofa seats.

**问题：**
- `dinned` → 错词。`dine` 的过去式是 **dined**，但更自然的说法是 `ate` 或直接用 `had dinner`。用 `dined` 过于正式。
- `dined in` → `in` 多余，直接说 `dined at your restaurant`。
- 时间位置：通常说 `around 6 pm` 放在动词后。

✅ **修改为：**  
**My friend and I dined at your restaurant around 6 pm, at a table near the window with two sofa seats.**

> 小提示：也可以改成更口语的 `had dinner`。

---

### 第三句
> ❌ The bag we left was a hand-size bag with several pink bows as decorations. The bag contained not only all my make-ups, but also my travel documents such as the passport and the driver's lience.

**问题：**
- `hand-size` → 通常说 **hand-sized**。
- `make-ups` → `makeup`（化妆品）是不可数名词，不加 s。
- `the passport / the driver's license` → 用 `my` 更自然，因为是你自己的证件。
- `lience` → 拼写错误，正确是 **license**。

✅ **修改为：**  
**The bag we left was a hand-sized bag with several pink bows as decorations. The bag contained not only all my makeup, but also my travel documents such as my passport and driver’s license.**

---

### 第四句（无误）
> ✅ If by any chance the bag is discovered, please contact me at 600-0000-0000.

这句写得很好，自然、礼貌。

---

### 第五句
> ❌ I appreciate your patience and support.

**问题：**  
这里没有“耐心”的问题（对方还没开始帮你找）。`patience` 不合适。`support` 也稍显正式。通常写 `appreciate your help` 或 `appreciation your assistance` 更好。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I appreciate your help.**

# 2

## 逐句讲解

### 第一句
> ❌ As a member of the company, I am writing to propose three suggestions for the company to reduce energy usings.

**问题：**
- `usings` → 这个词不存在。`use` 作名词时不可数，且这里应该用 **usage**（使用量/使用方式）。
- `for the company to reduce` → 可以，但 `for reducing` 更简洁。

✅ **修改为：**  
**As a member of the company, I am writing to propose three suggestions for reducing energy usage.**

> 讲解：`propose suggestions for doing something` 是固定搭配。

---

### 第二句
> ❌ Firstly, turing off the lights in unusable rooms can lead to more effective energy use.

**问题：**
- `turing` → 拼写错误，正确是 **turning**（双写 n + ing）。
- `unusable rooms` → “不可用的房间”意思奇怪。你想说的是 **unused rooms**（没人在用的房间）。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Firstly, turning off the lights in unused rooms can lead to more effective energy use.**

> 讲解：`unusable` = 无法使用的（比如坏了），`unused` = 未被使用的。

---

### 第三句
> ❌ Secondly, we as individuals should shut computers down after work. This is a good habit and let our computers to take a rest.

**问题：**
- 第一句没问题 ✅
- `let` → 主语是 `This`（单数），动词要加 **s**，变成 **lets**。
- `let our computers to take` → 错！`let` 后面**不加 to**，直接说 `let something do`。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Secondly, as individuals, we should shut down our computers after work. This is a good habit and lets our computers take a rest.**

> 讲解：`let sb/sth do`（没有 to）。`take a rest` 用于电脑有点拟人化，但在职场邮件里可以接受，很可爱。

---

### 第四句
> ❌ Last but not least, heating and air-conditioning should be scheduled in necessary hours. We can open our windows in early springs and late falls. Natrual wind only not can save our electricities but also is healthier.

**问题较多，我们拆开看：**

1. `scheduled in necessary hours` → 不自然。应该说 **scheduled for necessary hours** 或 **used only during necessary hours**。
2. `early springs / late falls` → 季节一般不加 s，除非特指“多个早春”。这里用 **early spring / late fall**。
3. `Natrual wind` → 拼写错误，**Natural**。
4. `only not can` → 严重语法错误。你想说“不仅……而且……”，正确是 **not only... but also...**，且语序为 `can not only save... but also be...`。
5. `our electricities` → `electricity` 不可数，不加 s。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Last but not least, heating and air-conditioning should be scheduled for necessary hours. We can open our windows in early spring and late fall. Natural wind can not only save electricity but is also healthier.**

> 讲解：`not only A but also B` 中，A 和 B 结构要平行。这里 `can not only save... but also is...` 稍有瑕疵，但口语写作中可接受。更完美是 `...but is also healthier`。

---

### 第五句
> ❌ Thank you for your considerations.

**问题：**
- `considerations` 不用复数。表示感谢对方“考虑”，用 **consideration**（不可数）。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Thank you for your consideration.**

---

# 3

## 逐句讲解

### 第一、二句（✅ 完全正确）
> Hello Susan,  
> How are you doing?

没问题，非常自然的口语开头。

---

### 第三句
> ❌ Thank you for inviting me for a movie night on Tuesday evening. I would love to go, however, my schedule really does not allow me to do so.

**问题：**
1. `inviting me for a movie night` → 介词 `for` 可以，但 `to` 更常见、更自然。口语中两种都能接受，但 `to` 更标准。
2. `I would love to go, however, my schedule...` → 逗号连接两个完整句子是错误的。`however` 是连接副词，不能像 `but` 那样只用逗号。应该用 **分号** 或者 **句号**。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Thank you for inviting me to a movie night on Tuesday evening. I would love to go; however, my schedule really does not allow me to do so.**

> 讲解：`I would love to go, but my schedule...` 也可以，那样 `but` 前面用逗号就对了。

---

### 第四句（✅ 完全正确）
> I am sorry that I cannot make it this Tuesday. My biology study group planned to go through the presentation one last time on Tuesday for the final project.

这一句写得很好！有一个极其微小的建议：

✅ **微调建议（非错误）：**  
**...for our final project.**（用 `our` 比 `the` 更自然，因为是你和组员的项目）

但 `the final project` 也完全正确，不需要改。

---

### 第五句
> ❌ I know you have been looking forward to it for a long time, so do I.

**问题：**
- `so do I` 的意思其实是“我也是”，但这里你想说的是“我和你一样期待”，英语里要用 **so have I**，因为前面是 `have been looking forward to`。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I know you have been looking forward to it for a long time, and so have I.**

> 讲解：  
> - 前面说 `I like coffee` → `so do I`（一般现在时）  
> - 前面说 `I have been waiting` → `so have I`（现在完成时）  
> - 前面说 `I went to Paris` → `so did I`（过去时）

---

### 第六句
> ❌ Would it be possible to reschedule it on weekends instead?

**问题：**
- `on weekends` 意思是“每周末/习惯性在周末”，这里你是指“改到**这个**周末”，用 `to the weekend` 或 `for the weekend`。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Would it be possible to reschedule it to the weekend instead?**

> 讲解：`reschedule something to + 时间` 表示“改到某个时间点”。

---

### 第七、八句（✅ 完全正确）
> Looking forward to your reply.  
> Love, Alice

完美。`Love` 用于好朋友之间很合适。

# 4

## 逐句讲解

### 第一句
> ❌ As a keen member of the sports clubs, I am writing to propose three suggestions for our party planning.

**问题：**
- `the sports clubs` → 如果你说的是某一个俱乐部，用 **the sports club**（单数）。如果俱乐部名字就叫“Sports Club”，也应该是单数。

✅ **修改为：**  
**As a keen member of the sports club, I am writing to propose three suggestions for our party planning.**

---

### 第二句
> ❌ Firstly, music is the catalyst for party vibe. We can prepare some city pop music which is chilling and happy. Anybody would love to dance and wave with the music.

**问题：**
1. `catalyst for party vibe` → `vibe` 通常说 **the vibe**（加 the）。另外 `catalyst for` 可以，但 `catalyst of` 更常见。
2. `which is chilling and happy` → `chilling` 意思是“令人害怕的/冰冷的”，你想说“轻松的/让人 chill 的”，应该用 **chill**（形容词）。
3. `Anybody` → 语法上没错，但 `Anyone` 更常用。
4. `dance and wave with the music` → `wave` 缺少宾语，应该说 `wave their hands`，或者直接用更地道的表达：`dance and move to the music`。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Firstly, music is the catalyst of the party vibe. We can prepare some city pop music, which is chill and happy. Anyone would love to dance and wave their hands to the music.**

> 小贴士：`chill` 作为形容词是口语用法，意思是“放松的、悠闲的”，在职场邮件中稍随意，但可以接受。

---

### 第三句
> ❌ Secondly, food is the key of the party. We should prepare common food such as spegatti to prevent any food allecies but guarantee to be tasty.

**问题：**
1. `the key of the party` → 介词错误。正确是 **the key to the party**（key to something）。
2. `spegatti` → 拼写错误，正确是 **spaghetti**。
3. `allecies` → 拼写错误，正确是 **allergies**。
4. `but guarantee to be tasty` → 语法混乱。你想说“既防止过敏，又要保证好吃”。当前写法不完整，需要明确主语。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Secondly, food is the key to the party. We should prepare common food such as spaghetti to prevent any food allergies, while also guaranteeing that it is tasty.**

> 讲解：`while also guaranteeing that it is tasty` 中的 `it` 指代 `food`。

---

### 第四句
> ❌ Last but not least, games are the good events after dinner. We can engage everybody by playing role-play board games.

**问题：**
- `the good events` → 特指“那些好的活动”有点奇怪。这里不需要 `the`，直接说 **good events**（泛指）。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Last but not least, games are good events after dinner. We can engage everybody by playing role-play board games.**

> 第二句 `role-play board games` 完全正确，很好！

---

### 第五句（✅ 正确）
> Thank you for your consideration.

正确。

✅ **修改为：**

> 讲解：`make someone feel + 形容词` 是常见结构。`and maybe even want to dance` 延续了 `make the guests` 的宾语，所以 `want` 不需要加 `to`（因为 `make someone do` 用原形动词）。

**Background music can make the guests feel chill and happy, and might even make them want to dance.**

✅ **修改为：**  
**Spaghetti and salads are dishes that are not only safe for people with allergies but also guaranteed to be delicious.**

> 讲解：`guaranteed + 形容词` 在某些非正式场合可见（如 `guaranteed fresh`），但 `guaranteed to be delicious` 更标准。

# 5

## 逐句讲解

### 第一句（✅ 基本正确）
> We came across your advertisement and think it is a perfect place for our friend's birthday party next Friday.

这一句完全正确，自然流畅。只有一个非常小的格式建议：

- `restaurant manager` → 在信的开头称呼中，通常首字母大写：**Restaurant Manager**

✅ 修改为：**Hello Restaurant Manager,**

---

### 第二句
> We have twenty people in total; however, we only found the option of a table for six online. Is it possible to have a big table that can serve all of us? If six is the maximum, we would like to book three in total.

**问题：**
- `serve all of us` → `serve` 通常用于“服务员服务顾客”或“食物供应”。对于“容纳/坐得下”一张桌子，更地道的词是 **seat**。

✅ 修改为：  
**Is it possible to have a big table that can seat all of us?**

> 讲解：`seat` 作动词 = 容纳……就坐。例如：`The table seats eight people.`

其他部分完美。`in total` 用了两次，但完全没问题，不算错误。

---

### 第三句
> We plan to start the dinner at 6 pm, and then play board games. We will bring our own cake, but hope you can freeze it until 8.

**问题：**
1. `start the dinner` → 可以去掉 `the`，说 **start dinner** 更自然（泛指“开始吃晚饭”）。
2. `freeze it until 8` → `freeze` 没错，但更准确地说，餐厅会帮你 **keep it in the freezer**（放入冰箱冷冻）。`freeze` 作为动词强调“使之冷冻”的动作，餐厅可能不会当场帮你冻，而是已经冻好的冰箱里存放。另外 `8` 最好写成 `8 pm`。

✅ 修改为：  
**We plan to start dinner at 6 pm, and then play board games. We will bring our own cake, but hope you can keep it in the freezer until 8 pm.**

> 讲解：`freeze` 和 `keep in the freezer` 的区别：  
> - `freeze the cake` = 把蛋糕冻起来（从室温到冷冻）  
> - `keep the cake in the freezer` = 把蛋糕（已经冷冻或需要冷藏）放在冰箱里保存

---

### 第四句
> ❌ Lastly, we would like to know whether this is a parking space in your building.

**问题：**
- `whether this is a parking space` → 语法错误。你想问“你们大楼里是否有停车位”，正确结构是 **whether there is a parking space**（用 `there is` 表示“存在”）。

✅ 修改为：  
**Lastly, we would like to know whether there is a parking space in your building.**

> 讲解：  
> - ❌ `whether this is a space` → 这个/这个是停车位吗？（奇怪）  
> - ✅ `whether there is a space` → 是否存在一个停车位？

第二句 `If not, where is the nearest parking lot?` 完全正确。

# 6

## 逐句讲解

### 第一、二句（✅ 完全正确）
> Hello Jim,  
> How are you doing?

没问题，很自然的口语开头。

---

### 第三句
> ❌ I was chosen to give a presentation at your company this Friday at 2 pm. I was told that it will take place at the center hall in the East building. Since you are more familiar with the location, I would like to ask a few questions.

**问题：**
- `at the center hall` → 对于“大厅/房间”内部，通常用 **in the center hall**。`at` 更多用于地点/地址（at the building）。
- `in the East building` → `in` 没问题，但后面 `of the East building` 会更清晰地表达“中心大厅是东楼的一部分”。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I was told that it will take place in the center hall of the East building.**

> 讲解：`in the hall of the building` 是标准搭配。

其他部分完全正确。`I was chosen to give a presentation` 写得很地道。

---

### 第四句
> ❌ Inside of the center hall, are there computers and projectors I can borrow? If not, are there any plugins I can use for my own equitments?

**问题：**
1. `Inside of the center hall` → 去掉 `of`。说 **Inside the center hall** 即可。`Inside of` 口语中偶尔出现，但标准写法不用 `of`。
2. `plugins` → 你想说的应该是 **plugs**（电源插座）。`plugin` 是“软件插件”或“可插入的硬件模块”，不是插座。
3. `equitments` → 拼写错误，正确是 **equipment**（不可数，不加 s）。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Inside the center hall, are there computers and projectors I can borrow? If not, are there any plugs I can use for my own equipment?**

> 讲解：`equipment` 永远不加 s。一件设备 = `a piece of equipment`。

---

### 第五句
> ❌ They did not give me the exact number of people that will come to the session. Do you know how many people roughly will participate in the event?

**问题：**
- `how many people roughly will participate` → 副词 `roughly` 的位置不自然。最好放在 `know` 后面或句子末尾：`know roughly how many people` 或 `how many people will participate, roughly`。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Do you know roughly how many people will participate in the event?**

> 或者：`Do you know how many people will participate in the event, roughly?`

`participate in the event` 完全正确。你也可以说 `attend the session` 避免重复，但不是错误。

---

### 第六句
> ❌ Looking forwards to seeing you there.

**问题：**
- `Looking forwards` → 错误。`look forward to` 中的 `forward` **没有 s**。正确是 **Looking forward**。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Looking forward to seeing you there.**

> 这是你之前写对过好几次的短语（比如电影邀约那篇的 `Looking forward to your reply`），这次可能是笔误。

# 7

## 逐句讲解

### 第一、二句（✅ 正确）
> Hello Jim,  
> How are you doing?

没问题。

---

### 第三句
> ❌ Congratulations on moving to your new house. I would love to help you moving furnitures. However, my schedule becomes really tight recently.

**问题：**
1. `help you moving` → 错误。`help` 的用法是 **help someone do something**（不加 `to` 也可以，但不用 `-ing`）。正确是 `help you move`。
2. `furnitures` → `furniture` 是不可数名词，**永远不加 s**。
3. `my schedule becomes really tight recently` → 时态问题。`recently` 表示“最近一段时间以来”，应该用现在完成时 **has become**，或者一般现在时 `is` 也可以，但 `becomes` 不自然。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I would love to help you move the furniture. However, my schedule has become really tight recently.**

> 或者更简单：`my schedule is really tight right now`（现在很紧）

---

### 第四句
> ❌ There is an emergency request from my work. I need to be on call during this weekend for answering clients' questions since my college Tim is sick.

**问题：**
1. `during this weekend` → 可以，但更自然的说法是 **this weekend**（不加 during）。
2. `for answering` → 表目的时，用 **to answer** 更地道。`for + -ing` 也可以，但 `to do` 更常见。
3. `college` → 你想说 **colleague**（同事）。`college` 是“大学”。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I need to be on call this weekend to answer clients' questions because my colleague Tim is sick.**

> `since` 也可以，但 `because` 更直接。

---

### 第五句
> ❌ Meanwhile, my car is sent to repaire due to an accident last night. It will not be available untill next week.

**问题：**
1. `is sent to repaire` → 两个错误：
   - 时态：车是被送去修，应该用过去时 **was sent**（昨晚发生的事）。
   - 拼写：`repaire` 应该是 **repair**。
2. `untill` → 拼写错误，正确是 **until**（一个 l）。

✅ **修改为：**  
**Meanwhile, my car was sent for repair due to an accident last night. It will not be available until next week.**

> 讲解：`sent for repair` 是固定搭配，`for repair` 表示“为了修理”。

---

### 第六句
> ❌ Lastly, unfortunately my daughter is sick. I need to priority my family's well-being before everything.

**问题：**
- `priority` 是名词，不能直接作动词。你想说 **prioritize**（优先考虑）。

✅ **修改为：**  
**I need to prioritize my family's well-being above everything else.**

> 讲解：`prioritize A above B` 或 `prioritize A over B` 表示“把 A 看得比 B 重要”。

---

### 第七句（✅ 正确）
> If you do not mind, would it be possible to wait for me for a week? I would definitely love to help you.

这一句写得很好，语气礼貌又真诚。`wait for me for a week` 完全正确。

## ❌ 语法错误

### 1. *from October 14 to October 30 inclusively*

**错误：** 日期跨度有问题。从 10 月 14 日到 10 月 30 日，总共是 17 天，这通常不叫 "two-week vacation"（两周 = 14 天）。

✅ **改正（两种方式）：**  
- 保持日期，改时间长度：*...request a **17-day** vacation...*  
- 保持两周，改日期：*...from October 14 to October **27** inclusively.*

根据你的写法，推荐：  
*...from October 14 to October **27** inclusively.*（这样正好 14 天）

---

### 2. *as well as to visit my family in home town*

**错误：** `home town` 是一个词：`hometown`。另外 `in hometown` 不自然，应该说 `in my hometown` 或 `back in my hometown`。

✅ **改正：**  
*...as well as to visit my family **in my hometown**.*

---

### 3. *and has been scheduled a year*

**错误：** 缺少介词。应该是 `scheduled **for** a year` 或 `scheduled a year **ago**`。

你想表达的是“这个活动一年前就安排好了”，所以应该是 `scheduled a year ago`。

✅ **改正：**  
*...and has been scheduled **a year ago**.*

## ⚠️ 表达不自然

### 4. *Alan will back up with my projects*

**问题：** 缺少动词。`will back up` 是正确的（back up 是动词短语），但 `back up with my projects` 不自然。应该说 `back me up on my projects` 或 `take over my projects`。

✅ **改正：**  
*...Alan will **back me up on** my projects.*  
或更清晰：*...Alan will **take over** my projects during my absence.*

### 5. *from 9 am to 18 pm*

✅ **改正（两种方式）：**  
- 12 小时制：*from 9 am to **6 pm***  
- 24 小时制：*from 9:00 to **18:00***（去掉 pm）

推荐：*from 9 am to 6 pm*
