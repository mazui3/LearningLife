Key Techniques for "Step-by-Step" Questions
Group Similar Actions: Instead of saying "Step 4 is... Step 5 is...", say "The middle steps involve various scrubbing techniques..." This makes you sound much more fluent and natural.

Focus on the "Why": Mentioning why you use the towel to turn off the tap (to avoid re-contamination) shows a higher level of English comprehension.

Use Imperative Verbs: Use strong action words like scrub, rinse, dry, and sanitize.

Sequencing Words: Even in a short time, use words like "Initially," "Subsequently," and "To conclude" to guide the listener through the steps.

Common Trap to Avoid
Don't get stuck on the technical names of hand parts (like "dorsum"). If you don't know the word, just say "the back of the hand." The examiner cares more about your fluency and clarity than your knowledge of anatomy!
